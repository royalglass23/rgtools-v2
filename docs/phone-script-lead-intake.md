# Phone Script — Lead Intake
> **Purpose:** Capture all 10 mandatory intake fields plus relevant optional questions.  
> **Tone:** Friendly, genuine, relaxed — like a helpful person, not a process.  
> **Tip:** These aren't lines to read out word for word. Use them as a guide and make it your own.

---

## Opening

**Inbound:**
> "Hey, thanks for calling Royal Glass! This is [your name] — how can I help you today?"

**Outbound (following up on a form, email, or calculator enquiry):**
> "Hi, is this [name]? It's [your name] calling from Royal Glass — I just saw your enquiry come through and thought I'd give you a quick ring. Is now an okay time to chat?"

---

## 1. Who They Are — Client Type + History
*This shapes everything. Find out early, keep it light.*

> "Just to get a feel for the project — is this for your own home, a rental, or is it more of a business or building job?"

*(Log: homeowner / investment landlord / business-builder-renovator)*

**If it sounds like they might know us already:**
> "Have you worked with us before or is this your first time getting in touch?"

*(Log: full exclusive / partial split / first-time enquiry)*

---

## 2. The Project — What & Where
*Open it up. Let them talk. You'll pick up project type and complexity just by listening.*

> "Awesome — so tell me a bit about what you're thinking. What's the project?"

*(Note project type: pool fence / balustrade / shower / handrail / other)*

> "And where's the job based? Which suburb are we talking?"

*(Enter suburb → distance auto-calculates)*

---

## 3. Where They're At — Consent Stage + Timeline
*Frame it around helping them plan, not ticking a box.*

> "Are you still in the early planning stage with this, or have you got consents underway or sorted already?"

*(Log: design / consent pending / fully approved)*

> "And is there a rough timeframe you're working towards for installation? Like a target month or season?"

*(Log: target installation timeline)*

---

## 4. Budget
*Lead with helpfulness. You're asking so you can guide them better.*

> "Do you have a ballpark budget in mind for the project? Totally fine if it's still rough — it just helps me point you in the right direction."

*(Note the number AND how they say it — relaxed vs. guarded tells you a lot)*

- **Comfortable and open:** note as flexible
- **Gives a specific number:** log budget band
- **Hesitant or evasive:** flag as price-sensitive

---

## 5. Other Quotes
*Matter-of-fact, no pressure.*

> "Have you had a chance to get quotes from anyone else yet, or are we the first you've reached out to?"

**If yes:**
> "Sweet — just so you know, we do offer a price-matching service if it ever comes to that. Totally up to you!"

*(Log: competing quotes yes/no, price match interest)*

---

## 6. Who's Making the Call
*Keep it casual — you're just figuring out who needs to be in the loop.*

> "Is this something you're deciding on yourself, or do you have a partner, builder, or anyone else involved in the decision?"

*(Log: sole decision maker / shared / third party)*

---

## 7. Package Interest
*Mention it as something worth knowing, not a pitch.*

> "Oh — one thing that's worth knowing about: we've got a package that bundles the glass, installation, and maintenance together. Would that be something you'd be keen to hear more about, or are you mainly after just the supply side right now?"

*(Log: combo package interest yes/no)*

---

## 8. Finish & Spec Preferences
*Helps gauge complexity. Keep it conversational.*

> "In terms of how it all looks at the end — do you have a really specific vision for the finish and details, or are you pretty open while things get worked out?"

*(Flag if they mention tight specs or lots of revisions — log as high-spec / complex)*

---

## Optional Follow-Ups: Homeowners
*Only ask if client type = homeowner or landlord*

> "Is it the home you're living in, or more of a rental or investment property?"

> "Random one — do you have any neighbours nearby who might be after glass work too? We sometimes work out group pricing when a couple of jobs are close together."

---

## Optional Follow-Ups: Business Clients
*Only ask if client type = builder / renovator / commercial*

> "How many glass projects does your team typically do in a year? Is this more of a one-off or an ongoing thing?"

> "Are you working with a regular glass supplier at the moment? Just curious if there are any gaps we might be able to help fill."

> "If it makes sense down the track, we can look at setting up ongoing pricing for repeat work — is that something that'd be useful for you?"

---

## Contact Details
*Gather these naturally — spread them through the conversation, don't dump them all at once.*

> "Can I grab your name for the file?"

> "And the best number to reach you on?" *(confirm if they called in)*

> "Do you have an email? I'll flick through any quotes or info to that."

---

## Wrapping Up

> "Perfect — that's really helpful, I've got a good picture of what you're after! We'll [send through a quote / get someone out to have a look / come back to you with some options] from here."

> "Is there anything else about the job you want to mention while I've got you?"

*(Any extra details → free text notes)*

> "Awesome, thanks so much [name] — we'll be in touch!"

## Field Reference Map
*Fill the intake form in after the call using this as your guide.*

| Intake Field | Where it comes from |
|---|---|
| Client name | Contact details |
| Phone | Inbound or confirmed |
| Email | Contact details |
| Location / suburb | "Which suburb are we talking?" |
| Project type | "Tell me about the project" |
| Client type | Section 1 — home / rental / business |
| Budget band | Section 4 — budget question |
| Consent status | Section 3 — planning stage |
| Complexity | Section 8 — finish & specs + project description |
| Price sensitivity read | Section 4 — how they respond to the budget question |
| Decision makers | Section 6 — who's involved |
| Source | "How'd you hear about us?" (ask if not obvious) |
| Previous history | Section 1 — first time / returning |
| Competing quotes | Section 5 — other quotes check |
| Combo interest | Section 7 — package mention |
| Consent (data) | Wrap-up — "happy for us to follow up?" |
| Free text | Anything extra at the end |
