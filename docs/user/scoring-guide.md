# Scoring Guide

The scoring system helps you prioritise which leads to act on first. Every lead gets a score out of 100 and a tier from A to D — A is the highest priority.

## How scoring works

When you save a lead, the scoring engine looks at your answers across 7 categories and adds up the points. The total determines the tier.

## Tier thresholds

| Tier | Score | Priority |
|------|-------|----------|
| **A** | 65 – 100 | Act immediately — high-value, ready-to-go lead |
| **B** | 42 – 64 | Strong lead — follow up soon |
| **C** | 20 – 41 | Moderate interest — nurture, lower urgency |
| **D** | 0 – 19 | Low priority or disqualified |

## Scoring categories

### 1. Client type (max 19 pts)

| Option | Points | Who this is |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Regular builder / Pool contractor with repeat orders | 19 | Existing trade client who sends repeat work |
| Existing / Partial Business Client | 15 | Business client who splits orders — room to grow the relationship |
| New prospective Business Client | 13 | New builder or renovation firm with strong potential |
| Owner-occupier / Homeowner | 9 | Individual homeowner, self-funded renovation or new build |
| Investment rental property landlord | 4 | Cost-driven, tends to price-shop heavily |

### 2. Budget band (max 19 pts)

| Option | Points |
|--------|--------|
| $50,000+ | 19 |
| $10,000 – $50,000 | 14 |
| $2,000 – $10,000 | 8 |
| Less than $2,000 | 3 |

### 3. Consent status (max 19 pts)

How far along is the client's building consent and project timeline?

| Option | Points | What it means |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| Both consents approved, install within 1–3 months | 19 | Ready to go — consent approved, construction finishing |
| Consent under review, install in 3–8 months | 13 | Mid-stage build, consent in progress |
| Early design only, install >8 months away | 7 | Land purchased, consent not yet submitted |
| No land / no drawing — purely price checking | 2 | Very early inquiry, no committed project yet |

### 4. Complexity (max 14 pts)

⚠️ Selecting "complex install" triggers a **blocker flag** — see below.

| Option | Points | What it means |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| Standard non-custom glass | 14 | Normal pool fence, balustrade, shower, handrail |
| Minor custom work | 9 | Some customisation but within standard production |
| Oversized / special laminated / switchable glass with complex install | 0 ⚑ | Beyond standard capability — triggers blocker flag |

### 5. Price-sensitivity read (max 14 pts)

⚠️ Selecting "high sensitivity" triggers a **blocker flag** — see below.

| Option | Points | What it means |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| Fast decision, reasonable on specs & pricing | 14 | Low friction — values quality, not just price |
| Average homeowner with normal negotiation | 9 | Some pushback, but manageable |
| Retired owner with strict fine-detail control, multiple competitor quotes & continuous price haggle | 0 ⚑ | High friction — triggers blocker flag |

### 6. Decision-makers (max 9 pts)

⚠️ Selecting "multi-layer board" triggers a **blocker flag** — see below.

| Option | Points | What it means |
|--------|--------|--------------|
| Single sole decision-maker | 9 | One person — fast decisions |
| 2–3 people, final call within 1–2 weeks | 6 | Small group, manageable timeline |
| Multi-layer board / division sign-off, decision >1 month | 0 ⚑ | Long decision cycle — triggers blocker flag |

### 7. Distance (max 6 pts) — auto-computed

Distance is calculated automatically from the Job Address. You don't select this manually.

| Band | Points |
|------|--------|
| Within 30 km | 6 |
| 30 – 80 km | 4 |
| Over 80 km | 2 |

## Blocker flags

Three answer options are designated "blocker low" — they earn 0 points and trigger the amber ⚑ flag:

| Answer | Category |
|--------|----------|
| Oversized / special laminated / switchable glass with complex install | Complexity |
| Retired owner with strict fine-detail control, multiple competitor quotes & continuous price haggle | Price-sensitivity |
| Multi-layer board / division sign-off, decision >1 month | Decision-makers |

### What happens when a blocker fires

The amber flag banner appears on the Score Panel and the result banner after saving. The tier is adjusted:

- **1 blocker selected** → tier is soft-demoted one step (A→B, B→C, C→D)
- **2 or more blockers selected** → tier is capped at C (regardless of raw score)

Example: A lead with a raw score of 70 (Tier A) that also has "complex install" selected will be shown as Tier B after the soft-demote.

The blocker flag doesn't mean you can't serve the lead — it's a signal to review carefully and price accordingly before committing resources.

---

## Tips

- The Score Panel updates live as you fill in the form — you can see the tier change before saving.
- If the amber flag fires, read the flag note carefully. It tells you which specific category triggered it.
- Tier D leads are not automatically rejected — they may still be worth a quick call to verify the details entered are accurate.
