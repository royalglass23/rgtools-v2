# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

RGTools serves Royal Glass operations staff, sales and estimating staff, managers, and administrators. These groups are equally important because they use different parts of the same operational workflow and depend on one another's data and follow-through.

Staff typically use the product during active office and operational work. They need to identify urgent work, understand the next action, update records, coordinate handoffs, and verify what has happened without searching across disconnected tools.

## Product Purpose

RGTools is Royal Glass's internal operations product. It brings together lead intake and scoring, client records, quote tracking and engagement, Work Orders, Producer Statement generation, administration, and supporting operational records.

Its future roadmap extends that operational flow into pre-install and invoicing workflows. Those future areas should feel like natural additions to one product rather than separate applications.

The product should make work easier in this order:

1. Show what needs attention.
2. Make the next action obvious.
3. Communicate clear status, including what is happening, what is blocked, and who owns it.
4. Preserve traceability through data sources, timestamps, history, and explanations.
5. Improve speed and consistency through predictable controls and minimal unnecessary steps.
6. Support exceptions with clear errors, manual overrides, and recovery paths.

Success means staff can identify urgent work without searching across modules, complete common workflows with less switching, miss fewer follow-ups, make fewer handoff errors, and learn the product with minimal instruction.

## Positioning

RGTools is Royal Glass's operational command centre, connecting leads, clients, quote tracking, Work Orders, Producer Statements, and future pre-install and invoicing workflows into clear priorities and next actions.

## Brand Personality

Precise, efficient, trustworthy.

The product should feel calm and capable during dense operational work. Its personality comes from reliable hierarchy, clear language, consistent behaviour, and restrained Royal Glass character rather than decoration.

Core references are:

- Linear for fast prioritisation and dense but calm workflows.
- Stripe Dashboard for clear data hierarchy and consistent forms.
- Architectural glazing drawings and specification sheets for precise structure and recognisable Royal Glass character.

## Anti-references

RGTools should not resemble a generic purple or blue SaaS dashboard. Avoid excessive glassmorphism, gradients, decorative animation, repeated identical card grids, oversized headings, low-density layouts, and low-contrast grey text.

Visual simplicity must never hide operational information or critical actions. Do not wrap every section in a rounded card or trade staff efficiency for decorative whitespace.

## Design Principles

1. Attention before overview. Urgent work and meaningful exceptions should be easier to find than passive reporting.
2. Action follows context. Show enough status, ownership, and history for staff to act confidently without opening several other systems.
3. One operational language. Repeated actions, statuses, forms, tables, and feedback should behave consistently across every module.
4. Dense, not crowded. Preserve the information staff need while using hierarchy, alignment, and progressive disclosure to control cognitive load.
5. Safe and traceable. Make sources, timestamps, automated recommendations, manual overrides, failures, and recovery paths understandable.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

RGTools targets WCAG 2.2 AA. Normal text, controls, placeholders, and focus indicators must meet the required contrast. All common workflows must support keyboard navigation with visible focus.

Motion must respect reduced-motion preferences and communicate state rather than decorate the interface. Status must remain understandable without colour alone. Dense tables and forms must remain readable and operable on desktop and responsive mobile web. Loading, validation, error, empty, success, and recovery states must be explicit and useful.
